Our Duck

The mallard is thought to be the most abundant and wide-ranging duck on Earth. They prefer calm, shallow water but can be found in almost any body of freshwater across Asia, Europe and North America. They are commonly found in wetlands.

The male birds (drakes) are the more distinctively coloured of the mallards with their iconic green heads and white neckbands, while females are brown-speckled in colour. They grow to about 65 centimeters in length and can weigh up to 1.4 kilograms.

Mallard groups rarely dive under the water, spending most of their time on the surface and living off invertebrates, fish, amphibians and a variety of plants. They also feed on grains and plants on land.

This species is the ancestor of most breeds of domestic ducks.
